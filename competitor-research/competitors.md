## Company 1: Heroku

- Website: https://www.heroku.com
- Founder(s): James Lindenbaum, Adam Wiggins, Orion Henry
- Location: San Francisco, USA
- Primary category: Platform Engineering / Internal Developer Platforms

### What they do
Heroku is an all-inclusive Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS) that takes care of everything related to devs from deploying, running, and scaling applications to complete elimination of server or infrastructure management.

### Target user (ICP)
Developers at startups and small teams that are looking for quick deployment of apps with minimum management of the infrastructure.

### Key features
- Deployment of applications using Git
- Runtimes and add-ons under management
- Scaling is automatic
- Monitoring and logging are integrated

### Pricing
Pricing based on usage, that is, paid  
https://www.heroku.com/pricing

### Positioning
“Modern apps. Instantly delivered.”

### Where they overlap with Clode
- Assimilation of deployment process
- DevOps for taking hire less.

### How Clode is different
- Little abstraction and lock-in with Clode
- Total automation and not hiding of cloud infrastructure is the goal of Clode

### DevRel angle
A majority of the PaaS tools settle for lack of long-term control in exchange for the speed of short-term. Clode gives an insight into how the teams can get the cloud setup done fast without any loss of ownership.

### Content hooks
1. “Why PaaS feels great on day 1 — and painful at scale”
2. “Automation vs. abstraction: what developers really need”
